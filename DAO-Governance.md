## 1. Introduction
Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs) are one of the most powerful innovations in Web3. They enable online communities and protocols to make decisions without centralized leadership.

---

## 2. What Is a DAO?
A DAO is a community-driven organization governed by smart contracts and token voting instead of traditional management.

It uses tools like:
- Discord for community  
- Governance forums  
- On-chain voting systems  

---

## 3. How DAO Governance Works
DAOs operate on proposals such as:
- Funding requests  
- Product upgrades  
- Treasury allocations  
- Rule changes  
- Strategic partnerships  

Members vote using governance tokens.

---

## 4. Voting Mechanisms
- **1 token = 1 vote**  
- Quadratic voting  
- Delegated voting  
- Multi-sig approvals  

---

## 5. DAO Tools
- Snapshot (off-chain voting)  
- Aragon  
- Safe multi-sig  
- Tally  
- Commonwealth forums  

---

## 6. Governance Tokens
These tokens grant:
- Voting rights  
- Proposal rights  
- Influence over treasury decisions  

---

## 7. Problems in DAO Governance
- Low participation  
- Whale dominance  
- Complex proposals  
- Voter fatigue  
- Coordination problems  

---

## 8. Improving DAO Governance
- Better tooling  
- Delegation  
- Incentive systems  
- Education for members  
- Clear proposal templates  

---

## 9. Examples of Major DAOs
- MakerDAO  
- Aave DAO  
- Uniswap DAO  
- ENS DAO  

---

## 10. Conclusion
DAO governance is evolving. As tooling improves and communities mature, DAOs will become more efficient, transparent, and democratic.
