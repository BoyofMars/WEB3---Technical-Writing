## 1. Introduction
Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) are among the most important financial innovations of this decade. Governments, regulators, and financial institutions are exploring digital versions of national currencies that can operate like crypto, but with state control.

This guide explains what CBDCs are and how they work.

---

## 2. What Is a CBDC?
A CBDC is a **digital form of national currency** issued and controlled by a central bank.

It is:
- Legal tender  
- Government-backed  
- Programmable  
- Potentially blockchain-based  

CBDCs aim to modernize payment systems while maintaining state oversight.

---

## 3. Why Countries Are Exploring CBDCs
- Faster domestic and cross-border payments  
- Reduced costs for settlements  
- Improved financial inclusion  
- Reduced fraud and cash management expenses  
- Greater transparency and traceability  
- Competition with cryptocurrencies and stablecoins

## 5. How CBDCs Work (Technical Overview)
CBDCs can run on:
- Permissioned blockchains  
- Hybrid distributed ledgers  
- Traditional centralized databases  

CBDCs may support:
- Smart contracts  
- Real-time monitoring  
- Identity-linked wallets  
- Offline payments (experimental)
